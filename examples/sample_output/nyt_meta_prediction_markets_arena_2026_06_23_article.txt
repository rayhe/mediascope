RECONSTRUCTED CONTENT (composite from secondary sources):

Mark Zuckerberg recently directed a small team at Meta to build a
smartphone app similar to Polymarket and Kalshi, according to the New York
Times, citing two employees with knowledge of the matter.

The app, internally referred to as "Arena," would function independently from
Meta's social networking apps — Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger.
It is described as a "top priority" for Zuckerberg and Meta.

The app is expected to rely on a video-game-like points system rather than
users wagering real money, though Meta has not ruled out eventually
incorporating real-money betting, according to one person familiar with the
plans.

Meta reportedly hopes to leverage its massive user base (3.5+ billion daily
active users across its apps) to drive adoption of the new service.

The effort is described as part of a broader push by Zuckerberg to identify
emerging online behaviors and build new products around them, as growth on
Meta's established platforms matures. Zuckerberg said in April that the
company could leverage leaner teams and AI tools to create more products,
after Meta announced layoffs of 10% of its staff.

Arena is one of several experimental projects currently under development.
Another initiative, dubbed "Meta Photos," focuses on creating new forms of
media using artificial intelligence.

The article also notes that prediction markets have surged in popularity,
with operators handling more than $50 billion in trades last year, and
volume already exceeding $130 billion this year. Combined monthly global
trading volume on Kalshi and Polymarket reached $24 billion in April,
compared to less than $5 billion in September 2025 (per Pew Research Center
analysis using data from The Block).

The report described the project as a "top priority" within Meta, but also
said the app could ultimately remain unreleased.

ADDITIONAL CONTEXT (from Engadget's editorial overlay, not in NYT original):

  used a points system and shut down in 2022 without gaining traction.
