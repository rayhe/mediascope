French antitrust watchdog orders Meta to resume talks with media groups over publishing fees

France's competition authority on Tuesday ordered Meta Platforms to resume negotiations with French media groups over fees for using their content, after media trade groups DVP and APIG filed a complaint.

The ruling comes amid a growing number of disputes between publishers and tech companies over neighboring rights, a form of copyright that allows media groups to demand payment when their content is used by online platforms.

DVP, which represents newspapers including Le Monde and Les Echos, and APIG, which represents online news publishers, had accused Meta of refusing to negotiate in good faith over publishing fees.

Meta said in a statement it would review the decision carefully.

The French watchdog has been active in enforcing neighboring rights rules, resulting in large fines for tech companies in recent years, particularly Alphabet's Google.

Benoit Coeure, the head of France's competition authority, said the decision aimed to ensure a fair negotiation process between platforms and publishers.
