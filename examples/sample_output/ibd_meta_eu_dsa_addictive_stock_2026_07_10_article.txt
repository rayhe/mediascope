# Meta Threatened With Major Fines In Europe Over 'Addictive' Features
# Source: Investor's Business Daily (investors.com)
# Date: July 10, 2026
# URL: https://www.investors.com/news/technology/meta-stock-additictive-features-eu/

Instagram and Facebook use "addictive" design features that put parent company Meta Platforms (META) in breach of European Union laws, regulators said Friday. Despite the report, Meta stock added on to a rally from Thursday in early morning trades.

Meta violated the EU's Digital Services Act by failing to adequately "assess the risks of its addictive design on the physical and mental wellbeing of users, including minors and vulnerable adults," according to the European Commission. The commission's report — which represent preliminary findings — called out features such as infinite scroll and autoplay and said Meta should consider limiting them.

If the preliminary findings are confirmed, Meta could face fines of up to 6% of revenue. Meta will have a chance to respond before the commission issues a final ruling.

Meta disagreed with the findings. A company spokesperson told the Wall Street Journal that Meta "share(s) the European Commission's commitment to providing teens with safe, positive online experiences."

The report from the European Commission adds to global scrutiny for the effects of social media on young users. Meta lost two high-profile court cases in March that some observers compared to a "big tobacco moment." The cases are among thousands that are challenging Meta, along with YouTube parent Alphabet (GOOGL), TikTok and Snap (SNAP).

In a separate trial scheduled for next month, state attorneys general are seeking $1.4 trillion in damages through a multistate lawsuit, Reuters recently reported.

Meanwhile, the U.K. approved a ban on social media for users younger than 16 last month, following the lead of Australia.

## Meta Stock Rises On AI News

At this point, investors appear to be shrugging off the latest legal risk for Meta's social media empire. Meta stock rose more than 3% (655.11) premarket trading on the stock market today.

The early gains extend a rally from Thursday. Meta closed 4.7% higher yesterday after it released its latest AI model. Chief Executive Mark Zuckerberg also confirmed that the company will consider selling some of it excess cloud capacity as a new line of business.

Meta stock is down 4.5% overall this year and down 20% from a record-high in August.
