A consolidation article weaving six distinct Meta narratives into a single "siege + ironic escalation" frame:

The article's thesis: Meta faces existential threats *to* its surveillance-funded business model, and its response is *even more* surveillance. The irony is the entire structural argument.

### 2.3 Proposed New Device: "Ironic Consolidation"

This article represents a framing pattern not yet classified in the 98-device taxonomy: **bundling multiple independent negative narratives into a single piece and drawing ironic connections between them.** Each story alone is a standard news item. Combined, they create a "pattern of corporate character" narrative that no single story could sustain.

This is distinct from:
- **Trend Bundling** (#27): which groups a company with peers to normalize/amplify
- **Recidivism Framing** (#97): which uses temporal markers ("once again")
- **Juxtaposition** (#24): which contrasts two items for effect

The consolidation device uses *volume* of simultaneous problems to create an impression of systemic crisis, and ironic connections between them (privacy lawsuits ↔ more surveillance) to create editorial commentary without explicit editorializing.

**Recommendation:** Track as a potential 99th device type pending 3+ additional examples.
